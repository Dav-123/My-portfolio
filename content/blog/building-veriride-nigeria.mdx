# Why I Am Building Veriride Nigeria

It started with a conversation with a friend who narrowly escaped a one-chance vehicle in Port Harcourt.

"One-chance" — the term sounds almost casual. But it describes a terrifying reality: commercial vehicles that pick up unsuspecting passengers, then rob, kidnap, or worse.

Nigeria loses thousands of people to this crime every year. And yet, we keep boarding unverified vehicles because we have no other choice.

## The Idea

What if every commercial vehicle had a verifiable digital identity? What if passengers could scan a QR code before boarding and know — in seconds — whether that vehicle and driver are registered, verified, and safe?

That's VeriRide Nigeria.

## What We Built

The platform has three main components:

- **Passenger App** (React Native): Scan, verify, and track rides in real-time
- **Driver Portal** (Next.js): Registration, document upload, and compliance dashboard  
- **Government Portal** (Next.js): Real-time fleet monitoring for FRSC and Nigeria Police Force

## The Technical Challenges

Building for Nigeria means building for low connectivity, low-end devices, and intermittent power. Every design decision was made with this in mind.

Am currently tackling this problem using an offline-first architecture, compressed QR payloads, and aggressive caching to make sure the app works even on a 2G connection.
